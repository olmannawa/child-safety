Anonymous Chat — Published Safety Standards (CSAE)

Published: 8 August 2025 — This page explains our child safety standards and how to report suspected child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE).

1. Overview

Our app is an anonymous, group-chat platform where groups are created inside the app and only group members can chat. We are committed to preventing and responding to child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE). These standards describe the policies, technical measures, reporting channels, and cooperation procedures we follow to protect children.

2. Scope & Applicability

These standards apply to:

  • All users of the app, including anonymous users and group owners.
  • All content shared via group chat, attachments, and user profiles.
  • All employees, contractors, and partners involved in the moderation and enforcement of the app.

3. Prohibited Content & Behavior

We strictly prohibit any content or conduct that involves or facilitates CSAE, including but not limited to:

  • Sexual content involving minors (under 18).*
  • Solicitation of minors for sexual purposes.
  • Sharing, producing, or distributing child sexual abuse images or videos.
  • Exploitation, grooming, or trafficking of minors.

*If you believe someone is in immediate danger, contact local law enforcement immediately before reporting through these channels.

4. Safety & Technical Measures

  • Age gating: Users must confirm they are 18+ to access adult groups. We display clear warnings on age-restricted groups.
  • Content moderation: Automated filters scan for known CSAE material hashes (via industry databases) and for patterns of grooming or sexual solicitation. Matches are actioned immediately.
  • Reporting tools: In-app "Report" buttons are available on group pages and messages so users can flag suspected CSAE quickly.
  • Rate limits & anti-abuse: Controls to limit mass messaging, repeated contact attempts, and suspicious account activity.
  • Attachment handling: All images and attachments flagged by filters are quarantined for review; known illegal material is preserved only for law enforcement requests and securely deleted after retention requirements are met.
  • Human review: Trained moderation staff review automated matches and reports. We provide escalation to senior moderators and legal counsel where needed.

5. How to Report Suspected CSAE

If you see content or behavior that indicates CSAE, please report it immediately using any of these channels:

1) In-app report: Open the message or group -> tap Report -> choose Sexual content involving a minor and add details.

2) Email: olmannawa@gmail.com (preferred for non-urgent reports). Include user IDs, group name, timestamps, and screenshots if possible.

3) Emergency / immediate danger: Contact local police or emergency services first and then notify us at safety@example.com.

When you file a report, we will acknowledge receipt and, where appropriate, escalate to law enforcement and child protection agencies.

6. Cooperation with Law Enforcement

  • We will preserve evidence and cooperate with lawful requests (search warrants, subpoenas) from law enforcement when CSAE is suspected.
  • We maintain a secure process for responding to emergency preservation and disclosure requests.
  • We may disclose account identifiers, message metadata, and content where legally required or to prevent harm.

7. Data Retention & Handling

  • We retain content and logs related to verified CSAE incidents for the minimum time necessary to comply with legal obligations and investigations.
  • Evidence that is subject to an active investigation will be retained securely and access-limited to authorized staff.

8. Enforcement & Consequences

Consequences for violating these standards include:

  • Immediate removal of content that violates CSAE policy.
  • Suspension or permanent banning of accounts involved in CSAE.
  • Referral to law enforcement where appropriate.

9. Staff Training & Prevention

  • Moderation staff receive regular training on identifying CSAE, reporting requirements, and trauma-informed handling of victims.
  • We maintain written escalation procedures and periodic audits of our safety systems.
